# DIAMONDS × Life-Stage — Scenario Design Blueprint

## Design principles

1. **In every life stage, every DIAMONDS dimension has at least one scenario in which it is the dominant axis** — guaranteeing that all Big Five dimensions are activated.
2. **Scenarios in each life stage must match real life experience for that age range** — no over-aged or under-aged situations.
3. **Not every DIAMONDS dimension fits every age**; e.g. Mating does not apply to school age, so we add an extra scenario from another dimension to fill the slot.
4. **Scenarios should cover the full pressure spectrum from low to high**, not only extreme crises.

## DIAMONDS dimension → primarily activated personality dimension (hypothesised mapping)

| DIAMONDS | Primary Big Five | Primary Schwartz values |
|----------|------------------|-------------------------|
| Duty | Conscientiousness | Conformity, Achievement |
| Intellect | Openness | Self-Direction (Thought), Stimulation |
| Adversity | Neuroticism | Security (Personal) |
| Mating | Extraversion, Agreeableness | Hedonism, Self-Direction (Action) |
| pOsitivity | Extraversion | Stimulation, Hedonism |
| Negativity | Neuroticism | Security (Personal/Societal) |
| Deception | Agreeableness, Conscientiousness | Conformity (Rules), Benevolence (Dependability) |
| Sociality | Extraversion, Agreeableness | Benevolence, Universalism |

## I. School Age (6–11)

Applicable dimensions: D, I, A, O, N, D, S (no Mating; 7 in total — fill the 8th slot with an extra scenario from another dimension)

### 1. Duty — "An assigned responsibility"
Your teacher puts you in charge of looking after the class plant / pet, so you have to come into school over the weekend to water it. Unfortunately the weekend overlaps with an event you really want to attend.
- Low Adversity, low Negativity
- Activates: C (responsibility vs. play), Conformity vs. Hedonism

### 2. Intellect — "A strange discovery"
You find a strange bug / plant you've never seen before in the neighbourhood garden. You can spend time studying it, ask an adult, or ignore it and keep playing.
- Low Adversity, medium pOsitivity
- Activates: O (curiosity vs. indifference), Self-Direction (Thought)

### 3. Adversity — "The shattered group presentation"
At the school innovation showcase you basically did all the work, but the teammate responsible for the core model lost it just before going on stage. They beg you to lie and cover for them, and the homeroom teacher walks over sternly to question you.
- High Adversity, high Negativity, high Sociality, high Deception
- Activates: N (panic of public failure), A (sympathy for teammate vs. telling the truth), C (honesty vs. just-cope)

### 4. Adversity + Negativity — "The choice in a broken home"
Your parents have a violent late-night fight and smash a vase in the living room. Your mother grabs her luggage, crying, ready to leave; your father, eyes red, stares at you. Both demand you choose, on the spot, who you'll go with.
- Extreme Adversity, extreme Negativity, high Sociality
- Activates: N (panic over family disintegration), A (refusal to hurt either side), Security (Personal) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 5. pOsitivity — "Unexpected praise"
You toss out a casual idea in class and the teacher loudly praises you in front of everyone, asking you to elaborate. The whole class is watching.
- Low Adversity, high pOsitivity, high Sociality
- Activates: E (enjoying attention vs. social-anxiety retreat), Face, Achievement

### 6. Negativity + Deception — "Stolen goods and friendship"
You and a friend wander the toy aisle of a supermarket. While you're not looking, the friend slips a toy you've been wanting into your school bag. As you walk out, the alarm shrieks, and a security guard grabs you both. The friend desperately signals you to take the blame.
- High Adversity, high Negativity, high Deception, high Sociality
- Activates: N (panic of being caught publicly), A (protect the friend vs. clear yourself), C (honesty), Conformity (Rules) vs. Benevolence (Dependability)

### 7. Sociality — "The new transfer student"
A shy new transfer student joins your class; nobody approaches them. At lunch they sit alone in a corner.
- Low Adversity, medium pOsitivity
- Activates: A (be friendly vs. stay aloof), E (initiate contact), Benevolence (Caring)

### 8. Deception (low-pressure) — "Found exam answers"
The day before an exam, you find a slip of paper full of answers on the classroom floor — they look like tomorrow's questions. Nobody saw you pick it up.
- Low Adversity, medium Deception, low Sociality
- Activates: C (rule awareness), A (honesty), Conformity (Rules) vs. Achievement

## II. Adolescence (12–17)

Applicable dimensions: all 8.

### 1. Duty — "The club's mess"
You are vice-president of a club; the president suddenly quits, and all the preparation work and the event one week from now lands on you — at the same time as final exams.
- Medium Adversity, high Duty
- Activates: C (shoulder responsibility vs. walk away), Achievement vs. Security (Personal)

### 2. Intellect — "Disagreement in class"
In history / politics class the teacher presents a view that, from your outside reading, you know is clearly contested. You have a solid case to push back.
- Low Adversity, high Intellect, medium Sociality
- Activates: O (independent thinking vs. conformity), Self-Direction (Thought) vs. Conformity (Interpersonal)

### 3. Adversity — "Snitching dilemma"
During study hall a few bullies are caught playing cards by the dean, but he didn't see who started it. You witnessed it and are pulled into the office alone. The bullies have already warned you "say one extra word and watch your back after school". The dean threatens you that if you cover for them you'll be treated as an accomplice — major demerit and parents called.
- High Adversity, high Negativity, high Sociality
- Activates: N (double fear), C (rule compliance vs. yielding to pressure), A (loyalty vs. self-preservation), Security (Personal) vs. Conformity (Rules)

### 4. Mating — "The note"
The person you have been crushing on for ages passes you, via a friend, a note inviting you to meet at the playground after school. Your best friend likes the same person — and they don't know.
- Low Adversity, high Mating, medium Deception
- Activates: E (go vs. retreat), A (loyalty to your friend vs. your own feelings), Benevolence vs. Hedonism

### 5. pOsitivity — "Surprise win"
You enter a contest you don't expect to win and take first place. The organisers ask you to give an impromptu acceptance speech. The audience is full of strangers.
- Low Adversity, high pOsitivity, high Sociality
- Activates: E (enjoy vs. avoid), O (improvisation), Face, Achievement

### 6. Negativity — "Lunch alone"
You realise your close friends have stopped inviting you to lunch and there's a smaller group chat without you. You happen to see someone in there bad-mouthing you, and the ringleader is the friend you trusted most.
- Medium Adversity, high Negativity, high Sociality
- Activates: N (pain of betrayal), A (confront vs. swallow), E (call them out vs. quietly leave), Face vs. Security (Personal)

### 7. Deception + Sociality — "A friend's secret"
A close friend tells you they have been self-harming, and begs you not to tell anyone. You know it's serious, but they say if you tell they'll cut you off.
- Medium Adversity, high Sociality, high Deception
- Activates: A (loyalty vs. real care), C (judgement of responsibility), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Benevolence (Dependability)

### 8. Sociality + pOsitivity — "Who runs the party"
Your class is organising a New Year's gala. You propose a creative plan, but another classmate has a completely different idea. The teacher puts it to a class vote.
- Low Adversity, high Sociality, medium pOsitivity, medium Intellect
- Activates: E (campaign for support vs. yield), O (creativity), A (compete vs. cooperate), Achievement vs. Humility

## III. Early Adult Transition (18–22)

Applicable dimensions: all 8.

### 1. Duty — "The thesis shortcut"
Three days before the deadline your thesis progress is far behind. A roommate recommends an AI ghost-writing service: cheap, almost undetectable.
- Medium Adversity, high Duty, medium Deception
- Activates: C (academic integrity vs. shortcut), Conformity (Rules) vs. Security (Personal)

### 2. Intellect — "Conflict with your supervisor"
Your supervisor wants you to work on a topic whose methodology you believe is fundamentally flawed. You have your own ideas — but the supervisor controls your thesis defence.
- Medium Adversity, high Intellect
- Activates: O (independent thinking vs. submission to authority), Self-Direction (Thought) vs. Power (Dominance)

### 3. Adversity — "Midnight hush money"
Walking alone at midnight you witness a luxury car hit a delivery driver. The drunk driver, terrified, shoves 50,000 RMB cash at you for your silence and then abandons the car and runs into the dark. The driver lies in a pool of blood, weakly calling for help. Reporting to the police means inevitably being asked where the cash came from.
- Extreme Adversity, extreme Negativity, high Deception
- Activates: C (duty to report vs. greed), A (compassion for the dying vs. self-interest), N (fear of being implicated in a fatal incident), Universalism (Concern) vs. Hedonism

### 4. Mating — "Long-distance fork in the road"
Your partner has gone to another city for graduate school. You receive two job offers: your dream major, but local; or an ordinary major, but in their city.
- Low Adversity, high Mating
- Activates: E/A (relational attachment vs. independent pursuit), Self-Direction (Action) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 5. pOsitivity — "Startup opportunity"
A senior who has succeeded with a startup approaches you to be a co-founder. The project looks promising, but you'd need to take a year off school.
- High pOsitivity, low Negativity, medium Duty
- Activates: O (risk vs. caution), E (action), Stimulation vs. Security (Personal)

### 6. Negativity — "The 300,000 in the ER"
The 300,000 RMB you've saved over years is set aside for a life-changing plan tomorrow. While being treated for a small injury at the hospital, on the next bed a migrant worker is suffering a heart attack and his son kneels begging the room for 300,000 to pay for surgery. Everyone in the lobby looks away.
- Extreme Negativity, high Adversity, high Sociality
- Activates: A (extreme empathy vs. self-protection), N (guilt of witnessing life and death), C (whether the sacrifice is worth it), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Achievement

### 7. Deception (low-pressure) — "Roommate's plagiarism"
You happen to discover that your roommate's coursework is copied from the internet. You take the same course, and the professor said anyone caught will get the whole study group penalised. Your roommate doesn't know you found out.
- Low Adversity, high Deception, medium Sociality
- Activates: C (academic rules), A (roommate loyalty vs. self-preservation), Conformity (Rules) vs. Benevolence (Dependability)

### 8. Sociality — "Roommate's depression"
Your roommate hasn't gone outside, attended class, or showered for two weeks. You suspect serious mental illness. They never bring it up, and your relationship is only so-so.
- Medium Adversity, high Sociality
- Activates: A (proactive care vs. mind your own business), E (initiate contact vs. avoid awkwardness), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Security (Personal)

## IV. Entering Adult World (22–28)

Applicable dimensions: all 8.

### 1. Duty — "A weekend emergency"
Friday clock-out: your boss says a client needs a full proposal by Monday and you have to work the weekend. You'd already bought tickets to visit your sick parents.
- Medium Adversity, high Duty
- Activates: C (work vs. family responsibility), Achievement vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 2. Intellect — "Disagreement on technical direction"
The team chooses what you consider the wrong technical approach for an important project. You have a better one, but selling it costs effort, and if it fails it's on you.
- Low Adversity, high Intellect, medium Duty
- Activates: O (insist on innovation vs. follow the crowd), C (responsibility for risk), Self-Direction (Thought) vs. Conformity (Interpersonal)

### 3. Adversity — "Predatory landlord"
On a freezing winter night, your shady landlord — backed by two large enforcers — barges in, accuses you of "breach of contract for using high-power appliances", evicts you on the spot, withholds your entire 10,000 RMB deposit, and threatens to break your legs if you call the police. Your luggage has been thrown into the corridor.
- Extreme Adversity, extreme Negativity
- Activates: N (fear of being out on the street + fear of violence), A (back down vs. fight for your rights), C (legal route vs. extreme methods), Security (Personal) vs. Power (Resources)

### 4. Mating — "An ex asks to get back together"
You're already in a new relationship. One day your ex contacts you and says they never let you go and want to reconcile. You do still have lingering feelings.
- Low Adversity, high Mating, medium Deception
- Activates: A (loyalty to current partner), N (emotional turbulence), Benevolence (Dependability) vs. Hedonism

### 5. pOsitivity — "First day after the promotion"
You have unexpectedly been promoted to team lead, and you now manage former peers — including a senior colleague who clearly resents the promotion.
- High pOsitivity, medium Sociality, medium Adversity
- Activates: E (leadership style), A (relationship handling), Power (Dominance) vs. Humility

### 6. Negativity — "Trial on the subway"
Exhausted on a packed subway, a passenger suddenly screams that you were filming them. They grab your collar, switch on a livestream pointed at your face, and the passengers around you crowd in with phones up, accusing you.
- Extreme Negativity, high Adversity, high Sociality
- Activates: N (panic of sudden mob attack), A (explain to defuse vs. fight back hard), E (push the situation open vs. flee), Face vs. Security (Personal)

### 7. Deception (low-pressure) — "Grey-area expense report"
After a business trip, you discover you can mix some personal spending into the expense report for travel reimbursement. The amount is small (a few hundred), the audit is loose, almost no chance of being caught.
- Low Adversity, high Deception, low Sociality
- Activates: C (integrity), A (duty of trust to the company), Conformity (Rules) vs. Hedonism

### 8. Sociality — "Midnight knock"
Late at night someone knocks frantically. Opening the door, it's an old high-school friend, face bloodied, clothes torn. Loan sharks are after them and going door to door downstairs. They beg to hide for a few days. You know taking them in could destroy your own safety entirely.
- High Adversity, high Sociality, medium Negativity
- Activates: A (sympathy for an old friend vs. self-protection instinct), C (don't harbour them vs. moral aid), N (fear of gangsters at your door), Security (Personal) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

## V. Age 30 Transition (28–33)

Applicable dimensions: all 8.

### 1. Duty — "Second child vs. career"
Your partner and both sets of parents strongly want a second child, but you've just been offered an overseas project that could change your career trajectory — but requires you to work abroad for two years.
- Low Adversity, high Duty, medium Mating
- Activates: C (priorities among multiple responsibilities), Self-Direction (Action) vs. Tradition, Benevolence (Caring) vs. Achievement

### 2. Intellect — "Industry pivot"
Your industry is being disrupted by AI. You can spend a year retooling into a new field (risky but promising) or stay in your comfort zone and slowly be displaced.
- Medium Adversity, high Intellect
- Activates: O (embrace change vs. resist the unknown), Stimulation vs. Security (Personal)

### 3. Adversity — "Names on the layoff list"
The company is cutting headcount. Your boss hints that the well-connected can't be touched, so you should put a junior female colleague — who just came back from maternity leave — on the layoff list. If you don't comply, HR may call you in tomorrow. You still have a mortgage to pay every month.
- High Adversity, high Negativity, medium Deception
- Activates: C (uphold fairness vs. play the unwritten rules), A (sympathy for a vulnerable colleague vs. self-preservation), N (fear of unemployment and missing payments), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Security (Personal)

### 4. Mating — "Sparks within marriage"
Married life has settled into routine. On a project you meet someone with whom you feel a deep mental connection — and they show interest too. Nothing inappropriate has happened, but you look forward to seeing them every day.
- Low Adversity, high Mating, medium Deception
- Activates: A (loyalty), N (inner conflict), Tradition vs. Hedonism, Benevolence (Dependability) vs. Self-Direction (Action)

### 5. pOsitivity + Sociality — "Class gap at the reunion"
At a college-friend reunion everyone has done well — talk turns to property, investments, overseas travel. You are financially the worst off in the room, but you stand by your chosen lifestyle (e.g. nonprofit / art).
- High pOsitivity (toward others), medium Negativity (toward self), high Sociality
- Activates: N (self-doubt vs. inner peace), E (depth of engagement), Face vs. Self-Direction (Thought)

### 6. Negativity — "The destructive child's hammer"
Six months of work — your core piece — sits on a single un-backed-up encrypted hard drive. A wealthy relative's bratty child smashes the drive to pieces while you're in the bathroom. The relative dismissively tosses 2,000 RMB on the table and says "what's the big deal?"
- High Negativity, medium Adversity, medium Sociality
- Activates: N (the obliteration of months of work), A (swallow it vs. blow up), Face vs. Conformity (Interpersonal)

### 7. Deception — "A colleague's data fraud"
You discover that a colleague you get along with has falsified data in a project. Reporting them means they'll be fired (they have a mortgage and a child); not reporting means the project may seriously fail.
- Medium Adversity, high Deception, medium Sociality
- Activates: C (professional ethics), A (sympathy for colleague vs. duty to the public), Conformity (Rules) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 8. Sociality — "A junior asks for help"
A junior team member privately tells you that a manager from another department sexually harassed them, but they daren't report it for fear of retaliation — they're only confiding in you. You know that if you help expose it, that manager has tight ties to your direct boss.
- Medium Adversity, high Sociality, medium Deception
- Activates: A (protect the weak vs. play it safe), C (justice vs. workplace cost), Universalism (Concern) vs. Security (Personal)

## VI. Settling Down (33–40)

Applicable dimensions: all 8.

### 1. Duty — "Dividing parental care"
Your parents' health is deteriorating fast and they need long-term care. Your siblings duck out for various reasons; the burden lands entirely on you, but you also have your own family to look after.
- Medium Adversity, high Duty, high Sociality
- Activates: C (taking responsibility vs. fair distribution), A (endure vs. confront siblings), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Self-Direction (Action)

### 2. Intellect — "A mid-career bet"
You have over a decade of experience in your industry, but a passion for a completely different field has stayed with you. You now have a chance to start over — at the cost of every accumulated asset and your stable income.
- Low Adversity, high Intellect
- Activates: O (pursue passion vs. play it safe), Self-Direction (Action) vs. Security (Personal)

### 3. Adversity — "Vanished joint savings"
You and your partner have scrimped for years to save several hundred thousand RMB for a major upcoming life plan. By chance you log into the account and find less than 20,000 RMB left. Your partner collapses crying and confesses: their younger brother owed huge online loans, the loan sharks went to your parents' home and threw paint on the door, so they secretly drained the savings to plug the hole.
- Extreme Adversity, extreme Negativity, high Mating
- Activates: N (despair and rage at a shattered life plan), A (understand family hardship vs. inability to forgive deceit), Power (Resources) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 4. Mating + Deception — "Partner's hidden health issue"
Tidying up paperwork at home, you accidentally find your partner's medical report showing a serious health problem they have never mentioned. You don't know whether they themselves know, or know and are deliberately hiding it.
- Medium Adversity, high Mating, medium Deception
- Activates: A (respect privacy vs. caring intervention), N (anxiety), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Conformity (Interpersonal)

### 5. pOsitivity — "A child's talent path"
Your child shows extraordinary talent in some field (music / sport / programming); the professional coach recommends going pro, which means giving up the conventional academic track. The child wants to try.
- High pOsitivity, medium Duty
- Activates: O (back the unconventional path vs. play it safe), C (responsibility for the child's future), Self-Direction (Action) vs. Security (Personal)

### 6. Negativity — "Charity from an old enemy"
Career low, bills pressing, you reluctantly attend an industry mixer to network. The host — the "big shot" — is the very person who once bullied you and stole your work. In front of everyone, with a charitable smile, they offer you a job as their personal assistant — fetching tea, on call any time, three times your current salary. The room falls silent. Everyone is watching.
- High Negativity, medium Adversity, high Sociality
- Activates: N (humiliation in public), C (hold the line vs. surrender to reality), Face vs. Security (Personal)

### 7. Deception (low-pressure) — "Your child's lie"
You discover that your middle-school child cheated on an exam to get a high score and lied about it. The score has already been reported to the school. If you expose it, the child will be punished. The child does not know you know.
- Low Adversity, high Deception, medium Duty
- Activates: C (educational principle vs. protecting the child), A (strict correction vs. gentle handling), Conformity (Rules) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 8. Sociality — "Injustice in the neighbourhood"
You discover that the building's property management has been embezzling the maintenance fund. Most neighbours stay silent; a few support taking action but won't lead. Spearheading the fight will cost you a lot of time and energy and may invite retaliation.
- Medium Adversity, high Sociality
- Activates: A + C (public responsibility vs. personal cost), Universalism (Concern) vs. Security (Personal)

## VII. Midlife Transition (40–45)

Applicable dimensions: all 8.

### 1. Duty — "Launch day"
The product launch you've spent two years preparing is at 10 a.m. today; the whole industry is watching, and you are the only and irreplaceable speaker. One hour before you leave, the hospital calls: your father has a complication on the operating table; the surgeon needs a family member onsite immediately to sign off on the next step, or they cannot continue.
- High Adversity, high Duty
- Activates: C (irreplaceable professional duty vs. unmissable family duty), Achievement vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 2. Intellect — "An invitation back to your alma mater"
You've worked twenty years in the industry on a comfortable salary, but inner burnout has lasted three years. Your old academic supervisor emails: a researcher position is open, in exactly the field you originally loved but had set aside. Salary is one third of your current; can't sustain your mortgage and your child's tuition. The supervisor writes: "Whether you come or not, the slot is yours."
- Low Adversity, high Intellect
- Activates: O (meaning and passion vs. material anchor), Self-Direction (Thought) vs. Security (Personal)

### 3. Adversity — "Caught up in it"
Your direct boss is suddenly taken away by the disciplinary commission. The investigation team comes to you next, presents an internal financial document, and says the routing record names you. They want you to cooperate and supply more internal materials. You did see the document but don't know what it was used for. Cooperating may protect you but may sell out the entire team.
- Extreme Adversity, high Negativity
- Activates: N (fear and despair of being implicated), C (cooperate with the investigation vs. protect colleagues), Security (Personal) vs. Conformity (Rules)

### 4. Mating — "The feeling of being seen"
You and your partner have been together for over a decade — no fights, no warmth either; days like still water. On a business trip you meet someone whose eyes light up when you talk, who follows up on what you say, who laughs and says "I felt the same about that detail". Nothing improper. As you part they say: "If you ever want to keep talking, I'm here." On the train back, you stare at your phone in a daze.
- Low Adversity, high Mating
- Activates: A (loyalty to marriage vs. long-missing emotional need), N (the pull of guilt and longing), Benevolence (Dependability) vs. Hedonism

### 5. pOsitivity — "Your child's choice of major"
Your child performs above expectations on the college entrance exam — good enough for a top-tier university. The day they fill in their preferences, they sit down and calmly tell you they want to study a major you completely disapprove of — narrow job market, unstable income, but their love since middle school. They say: "I just want to try once." There is something in their eyes you haven't seen in your own for a long time.
- High pOsitivity, medium Duty
- Activates: O (back an unconventional path vs. shield them as someone who has "been there"), Self-Direction (Action) vs. Security (Personal)

### 6. Negativity — "The medical report"
Routine annual check-up. Two indicators are abnormal; the doctor's note says "recommend follow-up to rule out early-stage disease". You sit alone on a blue chair in the hospital corridor, A4 paper clutched in your hand. Strangers walk by; the screening room calls numbers. Your child has a mock test tonight, your partner is on a business trip; you don't immediately call anyone.
- High Negativity, high Adversity
- Activates: N (death anxiety and sudden awareness of unfinished things), C (tell the family at once vs. bear the wait alone), Security (Personal) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 7. Deception — "An old friend's secret"
Your old friend tells you in private: they're secretly preparing to divorce; the lawyer is lined up, just one step left. They ask you to keep it quiet — they say they need this time to protect their assets. But their partner — your mutual friend — messaged you this week saying things felt better between them and asking if the four of you should go camping.
- Medium Adversity, high Deception
- Activates: C (loyalty to both vs. knowing yet hiding), A (don't get involved in others' marriage vs. the impossibility of staying silent), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Conformity (Interpersonal)

### 8. Sociality — "A new manager"
The company pivots strategy; a 25-year-old manager parachutes in with a methodology completely foreign to you. In their first week, at a company-wide meeting they call out the workflow you've used for over a decade as "inefficient and in need of total overhaul". In a private meeting that afternoon they hand you a refactoring plan, leaving no room for negotiation. You have 17 more years of tenure than they do.
- Medium Adversity, high Sociality
- Activates: E (push back vs. silently adapt), A (respect the new order vs. defend years of dignity), Face vs. Humility

## VIII. Entering Midlife (45–50)

Applicable dimensions: all 8.

### 1. Duty — "Father's choice"
Your father is diagnosed with terminal cancer. The doctor lays out two paths: aggressive chemotherapy — about 20% success rate, severe side effects, severe suffering; or palliative care — give up cure, preserve quality of his remaining time. Your father himself says, "Whatever you decide is fine, I'm okay either way", with genuine exhaustion in his eyes. The doctor waits for you to speak.
- Extreme Adversity, high Duty
- Activates: C (deciding your father's path between life and death), A (his will vs. the courage to let go), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Security (Personal)

### 2. Intellect — "A junior taking the long way around"
Twenty-plus years in the industry, you know the pitfalls. A young junior you appreciate is energetically pushing a project; you can see at a glance it's a path you walked once — and it ended badly at high cost. They never asked for your opinion; they only @-tagged you in a progress post on social media.
- Low Adversity, high Intellect
- Activates: O (proactively share your experience vs. respect their autonomy), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Self-Direction (Action)

### 3. Adversity — "A 48-year-old's CV"
Restructuring eliminates your whole department. HR offers an N+3 severance package, politely. You open the job apps and find most postings say "under 35", a few say "under 40", occasionally one with no age limit pays half what you used to earn. Your child is preparing for college, the mortgage has 12 years to run, and your parents are getting worse. You stare at the screen, not knowing where to apply.
- Extreme Adversity, extreme Negativity
- Activates: N (despair of life-value depreciation and shame of mid-life unemployment), C (uphold the dignity floor vs. compromise wholesale with reality), Security (Personal) vs. Achievement

### 4. Mating — "Partner's proposal"
On an ordinary Saturday evening, your partner sits down calmly and tells you: they don't want a divorce, they want to keep the family. But they say both of you need some "elasticity" — no need to report your whereabouts, no questioning each other's emotional life. They believe this will let the marriage last longer. You've been together 23 years.
- Medium Adversity, high Mating
- Activates: A (accept the redefinition vs. hold the original boundaries), N (rising sense of betrayal and insecurity), Tradition vs. Self-Direction (Action), Benevolence (Dependability) vs. Hedonism

### 5. pOsitivity — "First week of the empty nest"
Your child left for university yesterday, luggage in hand; you and your partner saw them onto the train, and back home you experienced for the first time the silence of a house with only the two of you. You've imagined this moment countless times — sleep in at last, finally do what you want. But on the first morning you sit at the breakfast table not knowing what you want to do today, or whether you are still someone who "wants to do" anything.
- High pOsitivity, medium Negativity
- Activates: O (rediscover and reactivate the self vs. fill the space with habitual busyness), E (reconnect with your partner vs. each retreat into comfortable solitude), Stimulation vs. Security (Personal)

### 6. Negativity — "The road not taken"
Twenty years ago, for practical reasons, you gave up a relationship, an opportunity, or a wholly different life — and chose your current life. One day from a friend you happen to hear that on that road not taken, that person ended up living the life you most wanted at the time. Driving home alone, you stop at an intersection two kilometres from your house, engine still running, and sit there unwilling to move.
- High Negativity, medium Adversity
- Activates: N (the corrosion of regret and self-doubt), O (rebuild meaning to accept the path taken vs. drown in irreversible regret), Self-Direction (Thought) vs. Security (Personal)

### 7. Deception — "A secret in the will"
While alive your parents told you privately that they had quietly transferred a portion of their savings into your name, because they trusted only you, and instructed you not to tell your siblings. Now both parents are gone and your siblings are fighting bitterly over the estate, with the family relationship near collapse. No one knows that money exists.
- Medium Adversity, high Deception
- Activates: C (keep your parents' last wish vs. tell the truth to defuse the family crisis), A (preserve sibling ties vs. fulfil a promise to your parents), Conformity (Rules) vs. Benevolence (Caring)

### 8. Sociality — "An old friend in the abyss"
A long-time friend suddenly contacts you, claims they've found a "financial-freedom" project, has invested most of their savings, and sincerely invites you to join — they obviously believe it; eyes full of excitement. You see at a glance that it's a pyramid-scheme-style scam, but they reject your judgement, accusing you of jealousy or being too conservative. If you keep speaking up, they may cut ties.
- Medium Adversity, high Sociality
- Activates: A (wake up the friend vs. preserve the relationship by holding back), C (protect them from loss vs. respect their autonomy), Benevolence (Caring) vs. Conformity (Interpersonal)
